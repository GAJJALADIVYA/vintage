    Vintage fashion refers to clothing and accessories that were made in a previous era, typically ranging from 20 to 100 years ago.       Wearing vintage is like wearing artit carries history, emotion, and individuality.

      • Natural Fabrics

        These fabrics were dominant before the advent of synthetic materials and were often prized for their quality and durability.

      • Natural Fabrics

        Popular from the 1920s to the 1950s, often called artificial silk. Used in dresses, blouses, and lightweight suits.

      • Blends

        Enhanced durability and reduced cost. Common in 1950s and 1960s coats and suits.

      • Specialty Fabrics

        A hallmark of Edwardian and 1920s evening wear, often used for capes and dresses. Resurged in the 1970s bohemian styles.

      • Prints and Patterns

        Common in cotton dresses from the 1930s to the 1950s. Seen in 1940s and 1950s skirts, suits, and jackets.

      • Opulent and Ornamental Fabrics

        Crisp, smooth fabric used in ball gowns and evening wear, especially in the 1950s. Characteristic for its slight sheen and "rustling" sound when moved.

      • Knitted and Stretch Fabrics

        Common in sweaters and fitted tops during the 1940s and 1950s. Often used in twinsets.

      • Sheer and Flowing Fabrics

        A semi-sheer, lightweight fabric with a crinkled texture. Used in 1930s and 1940s dresses for an elegant drape.

      • Heavy and Durable Fabrics

        Used in utilitarian clothing, particularly in workwear from the 1930s and 1940s. Later adapted into casual jackets and pants.
